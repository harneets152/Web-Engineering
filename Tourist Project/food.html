Creativity and food fusion is no stranger to Los Angeles cuisine as it’s known for incredible food. Much welcome cultural fusion in what they eat because they believe that there might be more interesting ways or takes in a specific cultural–oriented dish. Los Angeles is diverse in terms of food variety and caters to 12,459,000 residents. Knowing this here are a few spots that many residents typically go to:

List of Food Spots

  1. Broad Street Oyster Company

    2. Founded by Christopher Tompkins as a seafood mobile. Its origins trace back to Malibu during the summer of 2019 where it all started. They're known for their exquisite Lobster Rolls and their easy–going bar.

  2. Pasjoli

    3. Pasjoli is a French bistro that’s run by winning chef Dave Beran. They pay homage to French cuisine using the produce available in Southern California. They’re inspired by the Parisian markets and Pasjoli reflects Beran’s techniques and takes on French cuisine. Many people praise the hollowed–out brioche with chicken liver.

  3. Big Boi

    4. Big Boi was founded by Chef Barb Batiste after her sweet catering company B Sweet which was a dessert bar. Big Boi is focused on serving Filipino Food with slight variations and fusions with other cultures. A lot of foodies typically like her combination plates which consist of garlic rice and pancit with sisig and beef giniling.

  4. Tel Aviv Kosher Grill

    5. This establishment is known for it’s fast–paced, authentic Israeli cuisine. They offer vegetarian or meat dishes based on customer preference and they’re known for their shawarma to falafel. People recommend going for the Sabich.

  5. Mírame

    6. Founded by Michelin chef Joshua Gil and Matthew Egan. they bring Mexican cuisine with a twist. Their ingredients and flavors are portrayed or described as vibrant through their dishes. People recommend the fried Baja snapper with masa jus and the tostadas and tacos.

  6. Asanebo

    7. Founded by Chef Tetsuya Nakao who came to America in 1982 with his brother. They were the original chefs that helped start Matsuhisa in Beverly Hills and after establishing that restaurant, they ventured out and wanted to bring Japanese Gourmet Cuisine to Los Angeles. It’s re–opened and it’s the perfect spot to try the halibut yuzu or toro carpaccio.

  7. Angler

    8. It’s a sea–focused restaurant from Chef Joshua Skenes and Saison Hospitality. They specialize in sea–life cuisine and many people praise it for it’s attention to detail and exceptional service that they give.

  8. Crossroads Kitchen

    9. Crossroads Kitchen opened in March of 2013 and they serve Mediterranean meals that are vegan/ They are the first plant–based restaurant in town and not many people or customers assume that they’rre being served plant–based meals.

  9. Rosalind’s

    10. Owner Fekere Gebre–Mariam delivers soul–satisfying Ethiopian food. The mix of ground chiles with other spices makes the experience elevate to another level. Many people love injera with lentils.

  10. Dulan’s Soul Food Kitchen

    11. It’s a cafeteria–style dining experience that has opened since 1999. The owner Adolf Dulan, claims that he’s the King of Soul where believes that he brings an untapped experience compared to other food establishments.
